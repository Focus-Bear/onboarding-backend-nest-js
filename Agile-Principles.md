## Main difference between Scrum and Kanban## SCRUM

- it uses fixed length sprint sessions
- Defined roles , for example scrum master, product owner and team
- work planning is done at the beginning of project

## KANBAN
  
- No fixed length sprints
- tries to limit work in progress (focuses mostly on one task)
- Flexible for on going work (not as rigid)

## Identify one Agile principle you think will be most useful in your work.
  
- Small, but frequent releases: Agile's principle of allowing for small but frequent releases makes it perfect for reducing project burnout.
- It breaks down big, complicated tasks into smaller, managable and testable chunks; rather than one massive project at once (which can overwhelm developers).
